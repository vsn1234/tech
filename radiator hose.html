RADIATOR HOSE


1. Material: SNTHETIC RUBBER

The material used for car radiator hoses is typically synthetic rubber, often containing fibers for added strength.
The inner and outer layers of the hose are made of EPDM (Ethylene Propylene Diene Monomer) rubber, while the reinforcing
layer consists of polyester or aramid fibers. This combination provides high temperature and high pressure resistance,
making it suitable for the demanding environment of a car’s cooling system.

2. APPLICATION : USED IN ENGINES OF VEHICLES

The application of a radiator hose is to transport engine coolant, also known as antifreeze, between the engine and the radiator.
This helps maintain the engine’s optimal operating temperature by transferring heat away from the engine and dissipating it through
the radiator. The radiator hoses, typically made of rubber, are designed to withstand high temperatures and pressures within the engine
cooling system. They ensure the continuous circulation of coolant, preventing the engine from overheating and sustaining damage.
In most vehicles, there are two primary radiator hoses: the upper hose, which carries hot coolant from the engine to the radiator,
and the lower hose, which returns the cooled coolant back to the engine. Regular inspection and maintenance of radiator hoses are
essential to ensure their integrity and prevent potential engine damage due to overheating.

3. Purpose: TRANSPORT ENGINE COOLANT

The primary purpose of a radiator hose is to transport engine coolant, also known as antifreeze, between the engine and the radiator.
This helps maintain the engine’s optimal operating temperature by circulating the coolant, which absorbs excess heat from the engine
and releases it through the radiator. Radiator hoses also contribute to the overall functionality of the cooling system, ensuring that
the engine does not overheat and sustain damage. Regular inspection and maintenance of radiator hoses are essential to ensure their
integrity and prevent potential issues.
